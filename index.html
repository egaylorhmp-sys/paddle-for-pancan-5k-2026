Why We Paddle

"More than 158 mothers, daughters, fathers, sons, husbands, wives, sisters, brothers — who will hear the dreaded news every single day."

Pancreatic cancer takes 90 percent of its victims within five years and kills more people than breast cancer in the United States. It is the third leading cause of cancer-related death. We paddle to change those numbers — every stroke, every dollar, every year.

The Course

Out & Back to Williams Island

Launch from 1907 Suck Creek Road, paddle 1.5 miles upriver to scenic Williams Island, then return for the finish. Kayak, paddleboard, tandem kayak, or canoe — all welcome.

After the Race

Post-Race Lunch at River Drifters

Stick around and celebrate with fellow paddlers at River Drifters — a post-race tradition that brings the whole community together.

Competition

Three Divisions.
One River. One Cause.

Fastest time wins each division. All participants are eligible for raffle prizes donated by Chattanooga local businesses.

01

Adult Male

Open to all male paddlers. Solo kayak, SUP, or canoe. Timed from launch to finish.

02

Adult Female

Open to all female paddlers. Solo kayak, SUP, or canoe. Timed from launch to finish.

03

Tandem

Two-person craft. Any gender combination. Tandem kayak or canoe only.
